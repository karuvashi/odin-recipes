Chai

Description

My family are all big tea drinkers, but usually we don't have the time to make true spiced chai. We settle for a tea bag, some milk, and maybe some sugar if we're feeling indulgent. But every once in a while, we crave the real thing, or at least something close to it. This is the recipe I use, from @crossculturekev on tiktok, but feel free to make it your own!

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp loose leaf black tea - Assam is best
  • 1C water
  • 1C milk of your choice
  • 1/2 tsp crushed ginger or ginger paste
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1/2 inch cinnamon stick
  • 2 peppercorns
  • 1 clove - optional
  • your choice of sweetener

Steps

  1. Crush fresh ginger if using, cardamom, cinnamon, peppercorns, and clove. If using ginger paste, add to other ingredients once crushed.
  2. Add water to a pot that can hold at least 4 cups. Heat over medium high heat.
  3. Once the water is simmering, add the tea and the crushed spices from step 1.
  4. When the water comes to a rolling boil, add the milk.
  5. Stir once, then let simmer with the lid open until it reaches a rolling boil.
  6. Turn the heat to low and let simmer another 30-45 seconds. If you like, you can scoop ladlefuls and pour them back in to help enhance the color.
  7. Turn off the heat and add your sweetener. If this is your first time making this recipe, add a more conservative amount at this point; you can always add more once you taste it.
  8. Filter into a measuring cup or something else with a spout
  9. Find another empty cup or bowl that can accommodate the chai, preferably also with a spout. Pour the chai back and forth between the two cups/bowls a few times to aerate the chai. You should see some foam forming.
  10. Serve.
